# LinkedIn 120-Second Pitch — GrantGenie

**Format**: ~190 words · **Target pace**: 158 wpm → 72 s + 48 s of breath/emphasis = ~120 s total
**Tone**: Confident, specific, no jargon

---

> Small nonprofits lose funding to three predictable failure modes: they don't know the grants exist, they spend 80% of proposal time re-typing boilerplate, and they submit unfocused proposals that get declined.
>
> We built **GrantGenie** to fix that.
>
> It's a multi-tenant SaaS that helps a nonprofit **discover** only the grants they're eligible for, **draft** a complete proposal in under six seconds, and **track** every deadline with in-app and email reminders at fourteen, seven, and one day out.
>
> The secret sauce is grounding. Every sentence the AI writes is **cited to a document the org already uploaded** — past proposals, annual reports, program evaluations. No black box. No fabrication. If a claim can't be cited, it doesn't ship.
>
> The architecture is built for trust. Postgres row-level security makes cross-tenant data leaks structurally impossible. OpenID Connect for auth. Audit logs are append-only — Postgres triggers block any update. Every AI output is scored on relevance and faithfulness **before** a human ever sees it.
>
> Our target user is "Maya" — a grant writer at a $2M nonprofit, juggling 25 active applications. Today Maya spends 12 to 25 hours drafting one proposal. With GrantGenie, that's two to four hours — same quality, citations included.
>
> The U.S. philanthropic sector disburses over 500 billion dollars a year. We think most of that is leaving small nonprofits on the table. We're here to change that.
>
> **GrantGenie. Fund the missions that fund us all.**
>
> — OpenCode, July 2026
>
> #GrantTech #NonprofitTech #AI #ProductLaunch #LLM #CivicTech

## Variants for different audiences

**For technical audiences (CTO, eng leaders)** — replace closing with:

> "Built on Laravel 11, Angular 18, Python 3.12, Postgres + pgvector. Clean Architecture, transactional outbox, multi-model cost-aware router. Eval-gated, test-driven, OpenTelemetry from day one."

**For nonprofit executives (ED, Development Directors)** — replace opening with:

> "What if your grant writer could draft a funder-aligned proposal in four hours instead of twenty — with citations, with the funder's language, and with reminders so no deadline slips past?"

**For investors / partners** — replace the closer with:

> "TAM: 1.6M U.S. nonprofits, $500B in annual philanthropic flows. Pricing: $99–$499/mo per tenant + AI pass-through. AI cost is the cheapest adequate model first, automatically. Our differentiator is citation-grounded generation, not a faster prompt."

## Visual / formatting tips for LinkedIn

- **First 2 lines** are the only ones shown before "see more" — lead with the problem statement
- **Line breaks** every 1–2 sentences; LinkedIn collapses paragraphs
- **Hashtags**: 3–5 max, mix of broad (#AI, #NonprofitTech) and niche (#GrantTech, #CivicTech)
- **Image**: a 1:1 mock of the Discovery → Drafted → Tracked flow or a before/after time chart
- **CTA**: ask a question to drive comments ("What's the longest you've ever spent on a single grant proposal?")
- **Post timing**: Tuesday or Wednesday, 9–11am in your target audience's timezone
